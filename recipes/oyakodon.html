Oyakodon

Description

A bowl of fresh rice topped with tender chicken chunks and onions, simmered in a delicious dashi broth and topped with egg and spring onions.

This recipe is easy and quick to make and is a great example of the unique combination of sweet and savoury that is typical of Japanese cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 portion of chicken (thighs are traditional, although I prefer breast meat)
  • 1/2 white onion
  • A couple of thinly sliced spring onions, to garnish
  • A bowl of cooked rice
  • Soup ingredients:
    • Instant dashi powder, 1/3 a sachet
    • Sake, 2 Tbsp
    • Soy sauce (kikkoman is best), 2 Tbsp
    • Mirin (if unavailable, substitute with extra brown sugar), 1 Tbsp
    • Brown sugar, 1/2 Tbsp
    • Water, 1 Tbsp

Steps

  1. If you haven't cooked rice already, put some on now. It will probably be done by the time you are finished making the oyakodon.
  2. Mix up the soup ingredients in a jug.
  3. Prepare the other ingredients - cut up the onion, spring onions and chicken.
  4. Add the onion and chicken to a saucepan, and add the soup.
  5. Bring to the boil and then reduce to a simmer.
  6. Once the chicken is cooked through, crack an egg or two on top. Add the saucepan lid to steam the egg.
  7. Once the egg is cooked to your preference, get a bowl of rice and top with the finished oyakodon.
  8. Top with the thinly sliced spring onions.
  9. Scoff!
